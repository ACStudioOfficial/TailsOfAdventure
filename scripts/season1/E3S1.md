## Tails of Adventure (Episode 3, Season 1)
        
        
        *1 month later*
        *Tails wakes up and shows a montage of Tails’ morning routine with some light depressing ambient music*
        *Tails sits at a table and looks out over the beach*

        
//Also a note from me: Later on, I've tried to be more professional, but I guess that made it even worse, since **every** line now has it


__Tails__ (impatient): Another day off. Why am I still a mercenary if I don’t do anything? It’s getting boring.  
__Anton__ (curious): No news?  
__Tails__ (frustrated): Hey Anton. Still nothing.  
__Anton__ (sympathetic): Oh, c’mon. [Anton sits near Tails]  
__Tails__ (depressed): Yeah. It’s been over a month and still nothing. It’s getting depressing.  
__Anton__ (understanding): I understand... You know what continues to bug me?  
__Tails__ (intrigued): What?  
__Anton__ (analytical): Snow. Hear me out on this one: Snow used The Onion Routing, or TOR for short, and in theory, his computer must connect to Nook’s servers using a chain of servers, which must grant Snow full anonymity, but we caught him red-handed. Also, he almost instantly confessed that he did it.  
__Tails__ (thoughtful): I never thought about this...  
__Anton__ (confused): Something’s weird here. How could Nook track him down, and most importantly, why did Snow confess so easily?  
__Tails__ (conspiratorial): No. Math is not mathing here... What if... Snow “sacrificed” himself?  
__Anton__ (puzzled): What?  
__Tails__ (contemplative): No, listen. This would explain why we could catch him, based on that TOR is very secure, and why he confessed, but why he did it though...  
__Wey__ (casual): Hey guys [Wey notices Tails and Anton thinking] Listen, I don’t see you thinking that hard, especially you Tails since 2020.  
__Tails__ (nervous): Hey Wey. Nothing. We were just thinking about S...  
__Anton__ (trying to cover up): Steam Deck or Switch argument here. Nothing too crazy here [Anton nervously laugh]  
__Wey__ (suspicious): Okay? I don’t wanna participate in those arguments, since I just enjoy playing my favorite Pokémon games on Switch and nothing else.  
__Anton__ (anxious): Yeah. Do you have any news?  
__Wey__ (dismissive): Nope.  
__Tails__ (frustrated): UGH! Seriously?! Is there anything happening? I don’t want to sit here and do absolutely nothing...  
__Ashley__ (assertive): Oh, it must be you, MLC-771, right?  
__Tails__ (confused): Yes? Who are you?  
__Ashley__ (professional): Awesome. My name is Ashley Henderson MTS-770. I’ll be your team’s supervisor.  
__Tails__ (shocked): Wait, what?  
__Ashley__ (explaining): In accordance with Nook's internal regulations, it is necessary that each mercenary group is assigned a supervisor. This individual serves as the link between the team and Nook, facilitating mission directives, offering comprehensive support throughout all phases of operations, and ensuring effective communication and coordination. Any more questions?  
__Tails__ (neutral): No.  
__Ashley__ (businesslike): Great. So, as I can see everyone is here, but to be sure: Anton?  
__Anton__ (responding): It’s me.  
__Ashley__ (confirming): Alright. Wey?  
__Wey__ (acknowledging): Here.  
__Ashley__ (direct): Awesome. Lenka?  
__Anton__ (explaining): She’s not here.  
__Ashley__ (dismissing): That’s alright. She doesn't fit the criteria to become a mercenary anyway.  
__Anton__ (curious): Can I know why?  
__Ashley__ (detailing): Her IQ is less than 100, which is a no-go, but she can be a reserve mercenary with limited privileges, since her IQ is greater than 50.  
__Anton__ (accepting): That’s alright.  
__Ashley__ (continuing): Anyway, let me introduce you to your IDs. Tails already knows his. It’s MLC-771 or Mercenary Leader Charlie.  
__Tails__ (amused): Hehehehehe  
__Anton__ (complaining): Hey. Why he is a leader, not me?  
__Ashley__ (explaining): We decided that Tails would be a better leader.  
__Anton__ (grumbling): Okay, fine.
__Ashley__ (allocating roles): Now, Wey. Your ID is MSC-772 or Mercenary Secondary Charlie. Got it?  
__Wey__ (confirming): Got it.  
__Anton__ (arguing): Why is Wey secondary? I should be secondary.  
__Ashley__ (asserting): Secondary is determined by who’s more compatible with the leader, in this case, Tails. And now, Anton. Your ID is MBC-773 or Mercenary Backup Charlie.  
__Anton__ (outraged): BACKUP?! ARE YOU KIDDING ME?  
__Ashley__ (frustrated): Listen, Anton, I could dismiss you from the mercenary program if you continue complaining about your position here. In practice, it doesn’t really matter if you’re a backup or a leader. The leader can only change the plans in missions if the majority of his group agrees to his changed plan, and if he’s not available, secondary takes his place. Do you understand? Now, cut off that crying and let’s continue.  
__Anton__ (grumbling): Fine. What about Lenka?  
__Ashley__ (clarifying): Lenka won’t be involved for now, given that her privileges are significantly compromised compared to a regular mercenary. Her ID is jMBC-774 or Mercenary Backup Charlie Junior.  
__Anton__ (surprised): Junior? But she’s 19.  
__Ashley__ (explaining): The role of Junior Mercenary comes into play when the entire team is compromised, whether due to casualties or severe injuries. In such situations, she cannot actively participate in missions alongside the complete team. A Junior Mercenary is bound by specific limitations: she is prohibited from causing harm or taking lives during missions, and she is not permitted to carry or use any weapons whatsoever.  
__Tails__ (inquiring): Can I ask you a question? It may be inappropriate, but these definitions sound like they are generated by ChatGPT.  
__Ashley__ (confident): In theory, yes, they are generated by AI. In reality, it’s generated by Nate, a proprietary AI developed by Nook Inc to help the mercenary project. In this case, a special version of Nate for supervisors. You will get one too, but for mercenaries.  
__Tails__ (interested): And how we will get one?  
__Ashley__ (reassuring): Have you heard of the Neuralink implant? Think of it like that, but with AI integration. Don’t worry, the failure rate of installing Nate is lower than Appendectomy. It’s fully automated.  
__Tails__ (excited): That’s really awesome! When will we get one?  
__Ashley__ (informing): I will keep you all in touch. Usually, this happens on the same week as this interview
__Tails__ (curious): You know what happens next? You were a supervisor of another team?  
__Ashley__ (empathically): I like how you’re thinking, Tails. Yes, I was a leader of a “Blue Team”, but that team decided to break up and join other teams.  
__Tails__ (curious): Can we know more about that team?  
__Ashley__ (apologetic): According to the "Processing of Personal Data" agreement, I can't tell you. We take personal data even more seriously than Apple does. Sorry.  
__Tails__ (understanding): That’s okay.  
__Ashley__ (concluding): I think that’s it: I introduced myself, told your IDs, explained your roles, and introduced you Nate. Did I miss anything? Oh, is everyone comfortable to get Nate implant?  
__Wey__ (agreeing): Yes, why not.  
__Anton__ (enthusiastic): I’m cool with that.  
__Tails__ (eager): Can’t wait to have this Cortana thing.  
__Ashley__ (concluding): Alright. Everyone will get one, except Lenka, she’s limited. I guess that settles it down. I will keep you in touch. Have a nice day, “Light Blue” team.  
__Tails__ (casual): You too Ashley [Ashley leaves]
__Tails__ (exited): Did you hear that? We’re “Light Blue” team!  
__Anton__ (relieved): Yeah, finally. Anyway, I need to go. Bye [Anton leaves]  
__Wey__ (casual): Me too. I have stuff to do [Wey leaves]  
__Tails__ (closing): I guess I need to do too [Tails leaves]
